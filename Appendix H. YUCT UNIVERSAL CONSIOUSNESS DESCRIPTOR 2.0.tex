	\subsubsection{Extraction and Computation of UCD Parameters}
	For each study and each participant/condition, we extract or compute the following metrics:
	\begin{itemize}
		\item \textbf{Resonance parameter \(\gaR\):} From the power spectral density, we compute the spectral exponent \(\sigma\) (slope of the aperiodic component in log-log scale) and the mean gamma-band (30–45 Hz) coherence \(\gamma_{\text{coh}}\) over fronto-parietal regions. Using the definition in Eq.~\eqref{eq:gammaR_math}, we calculate \(\gaR = w_1 \gamma_{\text{coh}} + w_2 |\log_{10}(\sigma)|\). The weights \(w_1, w_2\) are initially set to 0.4 and 0.6 based on preliminary data (see Sec.~\ref{subsec:quantitative_assessment}), but can be optimized via meta-regression.
		\item \textbf{Dictionary complexity \(\alD\):} We compute multiscale entropy (MSE) \cite{Costa2005} over scales 1–20 and the Higuchi fractal dimension (HFD) \cite{Higuchi1988} from the EEG time series. Normalization to baseline (healthy awake) yields \(\alD = (\text{MSE}_{\text{norm}} + \text{HFD}_{\text{norm}})/2\).
		\item \textbf{Informational balance \(\beI\):} We construct functional connectivity graphs (using weighted phase lag index or coherence) and compute the variance of the degree distribution \(\text{Var}(D)\) and the ratio of global to local integration \(\text{IC}_{\text{global}}/\text{IC}_{\text{local}}\) (e.g., using graph-theoretic measures \cite{Stam2014, Kumar2024}). 	\subsection{Formal Definitions in Terms of Measurable EEG Metrics}
	\label{subsec:formal_defs}
	
	To provide a rigorous, empirically grounded bridge between the abstract UCD parameters and real neural data, we introduce the following operational definitions based on established quantitative EEG analysis techniques.
	
	\begin{definition}[Resonance Parameter \(\gaR\)]
		The parameter \(\gaR\) characterizes the degree of coherent resonance and long-range synchronization of neural ensembles. It is defined as a weighted combination of two metrics:
		\begin{equation}
			\gaR = w_1 \cdot \gamma_{\text{coh}} + w_2 \cdot \left|\log_{10}(\sigma)\right|,
			\label{eq:gammaR_math}
		\end{equation}
		where:
		\begin{itemize}
			\item \(\gamma_{\text{coh}}\) is the mean coherence in the gamma band (30–45 Hz) for the fronto-parietal network. Coherence is computed as \( \gamma_{\text{coh}} = \frac{|\langle S_{xy}(f) \rangle|^2}{\langle S_{xx}(f) \rangle \langle S_{yy}(f) \rangle} \), where \(S_{xy}\) is the cross-spectral density and \(S_{xx}, S_{yy}\) are the autospectra. Increased gamma coherence is a hallmark of focused attention and conscious processing \cite{Lutz2017, Garcia2021}.
			\item \(\sigma\) is the spectral exponent (slope of the aperiodic component of the EEG power spectrum in a log-log plot, i.e., \(P(f) \propto f^{-\sigma}\)). It is extracted by fitting the power spectrum after removing periodic oscillatory peaks. The spectral exponent reflects the balance between ordered (low-frequency) and chaotic (high-frequency) activity, and is a key marker of brain criticality \cite{Beggs2003, Chialvo2010}. Higher \(\sigma\) (steeper slope) corresponds to greater order and higher coordination efficiency.
			\item \(w_1, w_2\) are empirically determined weights (\(w_1 + w_2 = 1\)) chosen to maximize the separation between different states of consciousness (e.g., healthy controls vs. vegetative state). Preliminary analysis of the datasets described below yields \(w_1 \approx 0.4, w_2 \approx 0.6\).
		\end{itemize}
	\end{definition}
	
	\begin{definition}[Dictionary Complexity Parameter \(\alD\)]
		The parameter \(\alD\) reflects the richness and diversity of available neural patterns — the size and complexity of the neural "dictionary". It is defined as the average of two normalized complexity measures:
		\begin{equation}
			\alD = \frac{1}{2}\left(\text{MSE}_{\text{norm}} + \text{HFD}_{\text{norm}}\right),
			\label{eq:alphaD_math}
		\end{equation}
		where:
		\begin{itemize}
			\item \(\text{MSE}_{\text{norm}}\) is the normalized multiscale entropy (MSE) \cite{Costa2005}. MSE quantifies the complexity of a time series across multiple temporal scales. It is computed by coarse-graining the original signal and calculating the sample entropy for each scale. Higher MSE values indicate greater complexity and a richer repertoire of neural dynamics.
			\item \(\text{HFD}_{\text{norm}}\) is the normalized Higuchi fractal dimension \cite{Higuchi1988}. The Higuchi fractal dimension estimates the fractal dimension of a time series by analyzing its length as a function of scale. HFD ranges from 1 (simple curve) to 2 (maximum complexity). Studies have shown that HFD increases during meditation and cognitive engagement \cite{Kozhemyako2017}.
			\item Both measures are normalized to the range \([0,1]\) using the baseline resting-state condition of healthy controls.
		\end{itemize}
	\end{definition}
	
	\begin{definition}[Informational Balance Parameter \(\beI\)]
		The parameter \(\beI\) characterizes the balance and integration of information across the neural network. It is defined using functional connectivity measures:
		\begin{equation}
			\beI = \frac{1}{1 + \text{Var}(D)} \cdot \frac{\text{IC}_{\text{global}}}{\text{IC}_{\text{local}}},
			\label{eq:betaI_math}
		\end{equation}
		where:
		\begin{itemize}
			\item \(\text{Var}(D)\) is the variance of the degree distribution of the functional connectivity graph (nodes = EEG channels, edges = significant correlations or coherence). A low variance indicates a balanced, small-world architecture, which is associated with efficient information integration \cite{Stam2014}.
			\item \(\text{IC}_{\text{global}}\) and \(\text{IC}_{\text{local}}\) are measures of global and local integration, respectively. They can be derived from graph-theoretic metrics such as global efficiency and clustering coefficient, or from integrative connectivity indices \cite{Kumar2024}.
		\end{itemize}
	\end{definition}
